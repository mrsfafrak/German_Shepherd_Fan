History of the Breed

The German Shepherd breed was originally started by a German cavalry officer named Captain Max von Stephanitz in the early 1800â€™s. He wanted to develop the ideal German herder canine by crossing different breeds from various areas of the country so it could be used in military and police work.

As a result the German Shepherd ancestor was developed with the characteristics of loyalty, protectiveness, courage, confidence, immense intellect, and bravery.

Appearance

German Shepherds have a very strong, musculuar, and agile build. Their upright, pointy ears add to their alert nature while their longer snouts are also very characteristic of the breed.

The American Kennel Club (AKC) recgonizes the following standard colors for the German Shepherd breed:

  • Bi-color
  • Black
  • Black & Cream
  • Black & Red
  • Black & Silver
  • Black & Tan
  • Blue
  • Gray
  • Liver
  • Sable
  • White
